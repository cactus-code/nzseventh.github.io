About The Leader

1935 - Present Day

Toby Govin-Fowler was born in India, as the illegitimate son of Ghandi. After a flawless education, Toby rose up the ranks of the Indian revolutionaries before his entire regiment was captured by a British recon force led by our deputy leader, Lewis Grey. During his years of imprisonment he wrote a book of hate of our current “Democracy”- titled On Pigment. Toby later was personally released by Major-General Grey, and then reformed under the ideology of the British democracy. He then re-wrote On Pigment under his new thoughts on democracy, and left Great Britain to spread the word in back in India. After many years of failure in his home country: Guru Toby Govin-Fowler attempts to spread “the word” in New Zealand and reunites with old friend Major-General Lewis Grey (Now New-Zealand's chief of defence). They united alongside Lewis’s newfound dicky chums and formed the glorious NZ Seventh party as we know it today.
