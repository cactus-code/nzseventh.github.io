About The Deputy Leader

1926 - Present Day

Lewis Grey, was born in Windsor Castle as the next rightful king of Great Britain. During a family occasion he was however mistaken for the child of Winston Churchill and an unintentional child-swap occurred. Lewis Grey (despite perfect education) was forced into a Military occupation and soon had a legendary reputation in the British army. His last operation as part of the British empire was a night assault on the famed “Guru Toby’s” unit. It was an outstanding success and Lewis immediately retired, Guru Govin-Fowler was then reformed by Lewis and was released. He then travelled around the globe until settling down as the New-Zealand unofficial chief of defence, and taking in Robert Gaudin under his wing. He soon re-united with Toby Govin-Fowler (a rather popular guru at the time) and then joined NZ Seventh and helped form what we know it as today.
